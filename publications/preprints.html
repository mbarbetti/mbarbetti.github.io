    \(J/\psi\) and \(D^0\) production in \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\) \(\rm{PbNe}\) collisions
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 21 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    The first measurement of \(J/\psi\) and \(D^0\) production in \(\rm{PbNe}\) collisions by the LHCb experiment in its fixed-target configuration is reported. The production of \(J/\psi\) and \(D^0\) mesons is studied with a beam of lead ions with an energy of \(2.5~\rm{TeV}\) per nucleon colliding on gaseous neon targets at rest, corresponding to a nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\). The \(J/\psi D^0\) production cross-section ratio is studied as a function of rapidity, transverse momentum and collision centrality. These data are compared with measurements from \(p\rm{Ne}\) collisions at the same energy. No anomalous \(J/\psi\) suppression that could indicate the formation of a deconfined medium is observed.

  2. arXiv
    Charmonium production in \(p\rm{Ne}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\)
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 21 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    The measurement of charmonium states produced in proton-neon (\(p\rm{Ne}\)) collisions by the LHCb experiment in its fixed-target configuration is presented. The production of \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) mesons is studied with a beam of \(2.5~\rm{TeV}\) protons colliding on gaseous neon targets at rest, corresponding to a nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\). The data sample corresponds to an integrated luminosity of \(21.7 \pm 1.4~\rm{nb}^{-1}\). The \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) hadrons are reconstructed in \(\mu^+ \mu^-\) final states. The \(J/\psi\) production cross-section per target nucleon in the centre-of-mass rapidity range \(y^* \in [-2.29, 0]\) is found to be \((506 \pm 8 \pm 25)~\rm{nb}/{nucleon}\). The \(\psi(2S)\) to \(J/\psi\) relative production rate is found to be \((1.67 \pm 0.27 \pm 0.10)\%\) in good agreement with other measurements involving beam and target nuclei of similar sizes.

  3. arXiv
    Open charm production and asymmetry in \(p\rm{Ne}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\)
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 21 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    A measurement of D0 meson production by the LHCb experiment in its fixed-target configuration is presented. The production of \(D^0\) mesons is studied with a beam of \(2.5~\rm{TeV}\) protons colliding on a gaseous neon target at rest, corresponding to a nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 68.5~\rm{GeV}\). The \(D^0\) and \(\overline{D}^0\) production cross-section in \(p\rm{Ne}\) collisions in the centre-of-mass rapidity range \(y^* \in [-2.29, 0]\) is found to be \(\sigma^{y^* \in [-2.29, 0]}_{D^0} = (48.2 \pm 0.3 \pm 4.5)~\mu\rm{b}/\rm{nucleon}\) where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is systematic. The \(D^0 - \overline{D}^0\) production asymmetry is also evaluated and suggests a negative trend at large negative \(y^*\). The considered models do not account precisely for all the features observed in the LHCb data, but theoretical predictions including \(1\%\) intrinsic charm and \(10\%\) recombination contributions better describe the data than the other models considered.

  4. arXiv
    Long-lived particle reconstruction downstream of the LHCb magnet
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 20 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    Charged-particle trajectories are usually reconstructed with the LHCb detector using combined information from the tracking devices placed upstream and downstream of the \(4~\rm{Tm}\) dipole magnet. Trajectories reconstructed using only information from the tracker downstream of the dipole magnet, which are referred to as T tracks, have not been used for physics analysis to date due to their limited momentum resolution. The challenges of the reconstruction of long-lived particles using T tracks for use in physics analyses are discussed and a solution is proposed. The feasibility of the experimental technique is demonstrated by reconstructing samples of long-lived \(\Lambda\) and \(K^0_S\) hadrons decaying between \(6.0\) and \(7.6\) metres downstream of the proton-proton collision point. The long-lived hadrons are selected using a data sample recorded between 2015 and 2018, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of about \(6~\rm{fb}^{-1}\). These results open an opportunity to further extend the physics reach of the LHCb experiment.

    Searches for the rare hadronic decays \(B^0 \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\) and \(B^0_{s} \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\)
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 16 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    Searches for the rare hadronic decays \(B^0 \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\) and \(B^0_{s} \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\) are performed using proton-proton collision data recorded by the LHCb experiment and corresponding to an integrated luminosity of \(9~\rm{fb}^{-1}\). Significances of \(9.3\sigma\) and \(4.0\sigma\), including statistical and systematic uncertainties, are obtained for the \(B^0 \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\) and \(B^0_{s} \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}\) signals, respectively. The branching fractions are measured relative to the topologically similar normalisation decays \(B^0 \to J/\psi (\to p\bar{p}) K^{*0} (\to K^+ \pi^-)\) and \(B^0_s \to J/\psi (\to p\bar{p}) \phi (\to K^+ K^-)\). The branching fractions are measured to be \(\mathcal{B}(B^0 \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}) = (2.2 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.1 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-8}\) and \(\mathcal{B}(B^0_{s} \rightarrow p\bar{p}p\bar{p}) = (2.3 \pm 1.0 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-8}\). In these measurements, the first uncertainty is statistical, the second is systematic and the third one is due to the external branching fraction of the normalisation channel.

    First observation of the \(B^+ \rightarrow D_s^+ D_s^- K^+\) decay
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 9 Nov 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    The \(B^+ \rightarrow D_s^+ D_s^- K^+\) decay is observed for the first time using proton-proton collision data collected by the LHCb detector at centre-of-mass energies of \(7\), \(8\) and \(13~\rm{TeV}\), corresponding to an integrated luminosity of \(9~\rm{fb}^{-1}\). This measurement fills an experimental gap in the knowledge of the family of Cabibbo-favoured \(\bar{b} \to \bar{c} c \bar{s}\) transitions and opens the path for unique studies of spectroscopy in future.

  8. arXiv

    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 27 Oct 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    An amplitude analysis of the \(B^+\to D_s^+ D_s^- K^+\) decay is carried out to study for the first time its intermediate resonant contributions, using proton-proton collision data collected with the LHCb detector at centre-of-mass energies of \(7\), \(8\) and \(13~\rm{TeV}\).   15. arXiv
    Amplitude analysis of the \(D_s^+ \to \pi^- \pi^+ \pi^+\) decay
    LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 20 Sep 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    A Dalitz plot analysis of the \(D_s^+ \to \pi^- \pi^+ \pi^+\) decay is presented.     LHCb Collaboration
    arXiv preprint â€“ Submitted on 7 Sep 2022
    AbsarXivCDS

    The time-integrated \(CP\) asymmetry in the Cabibbo-suppressed decay \(D^0\to K^- K^+\) is measured using proton-proton collision data, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of \(5.7~\rm{fb}^{-1}\) collected at a center-of-mass energy of \(13~\rm{TeV}\) with the LHCb detector. The \(D^0\) mesons are required to originate from promptly produced \(D^{*+} \to D^0 \pi^+\) decays and the charge of the companion pion is used to determine the flavor of the charm meson at production. The time-integrated \(CP\) asymmetry is measured to be \(\mathcal{A}_{CP}(K^- K^+) = [6.8 \pm 5.4~\rm{(stat)} \pm 1.6~\rm{(syst)}] \times 10^{-4}\). The direct \(CP\) asymmetries in \(D^0\to K^- K^+\) and \(D^0\to \pi^- \pi^+\) decays, \(a^d_{K^-K^+}\) and \(a^d_{\pi^- \pi^+}\), are derived by combining \(\mathcal{A}_{CP}(K^- K^+)\) with the time-integrated \(CP\) asymmetry difference, \(\Delta \mathcal{A}_{CP} = \mathcal{A}_{CP}(K^- K^+) - \mathcal{A}_{CP} (\pi^- \pi^+)\), giving \(a^d_{K^- K^+} = (7.7 \pm 5.7) \times 10^{-4}\), \(a^d_{\pi^- \pi^+} = (23.2 \pm 6.1) \times 10^{-4}\), with a correlation coefficient corresponding to \(\rho = 0.88\). The compatibility of these results with \(CP\) symmetry is \(1.4\) and \(3.8\) standard deviations for \(D^0\to K^- K^+\) and \(D^0\to \pi^- \pi^+\) decays, respectively. This is the first evidence for direct \(CP\) violation in a specific \(D^0\) decay.
